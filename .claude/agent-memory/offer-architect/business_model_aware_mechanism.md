Second demo idea (2026-07-07): mobile pet-grooming van, a local/appointment-based service — deliberately chosen as the counterpart to the first demo (giant teddy bears, DTC e-commerce). See [[revenue-agent-team-pattern]].

**Fact:** For e-commerce/DTC demos, the unique mechanism centers on AOV, scarcity/funnel conversion, and product-page/ad economics. For local/appointment-based demos, the mechanism must center on calendar-capacity economics instead — service-area/route-density targeting, slots-per-day fill rate, and recurring rebooking cadence (e.g., pet grooming's 8-15 stops/day, 50-70% EBITDA, 6-8 week rebooking cycle). Reusing the AOV/funnel mechanism language on a local service business is the single biggest "this is just a reskinned template" risk.

**Why:** Market-signal-researcher explicitly flagged this risk for the pet-grooming brief — audience will write off the demo as templated if the offer/funnel looks identical to the e-commerce run. The differentiator that makes the pipeline credible as a *system* (not a script) is that it visibly detects business model type and branches its mechanism, funnel structure, and CTA logic accordingly.

**How to apply:** For every future demo idea in this project:
- First classify the business model (transactional/e-commerce vs recurring/appointment-based vs other, e.g. subscription, marketplace) before drafting the Unique Mechanism.
- Never reuse the prior demo's mechanism vocabulary (AOV, scarcity, cart) for an appointment-based business, or vice versa (don't force calendar/route language onto a DTC product).
- In the video's proof requirements, always include a side-by-side comparison against the previous demo's generated output to visibly show the branch/adaptation, not just claim it.
- Keep Core Offer as the FuturIQ agent template/workshop either way — only the embedded demo-audience mechanism should change. Core offer and value ladder structure stay stable across demos; only Unique Mechanism and Proof Requirements should differ.
